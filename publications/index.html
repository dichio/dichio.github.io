  2. arXiv
    Statistical models of complex brain networks: a maximum entropy approach
    Dichio, Vito, and Fallani, Fabrizio De Vico
    arXiv preprint arXiv:2209.05829 2022
    Abs Bib HTML

    We focus on Exponential Random Graph Models (ERGMs) and thir applications to network neuroscience. ERGMs are a family of maxent inference models designed for network data. These models can be utilized to characterize the state of a network, distinguish between different states, and forecast future outcomes. We present a brief overview of the method, examine current applications in brain networks, and provide a critical discussion.

2021

  1. JSTAT
    Inferring epistasis from genomic data with comparable mutation and outcrossing rate
    Zeng, Hong-Li, Mauri, Eugenio, Dichio, Vito, Cocco, Simona, Monasson, Remi, and Aurell, Erik
    Journal of Statistical Mechanics: Theory and Experiment 2021
    Abs Bib HTML

    We address the problem of inferring fitness in an evolutionary dynamics from multiple snapshots of the genotype distribution in a population that is in a quasi-linkage equilibrium regime. We extend the current state-of-the-art methods and leverage a recently proposed technique that uses a Gaussian approximation for the probability distribution of the genome. The results are validated through in silico experiments.

2020

  1. PNAS
    Global analysis of more than 50,000 SARS-CoV-2 genomes reveals epistasis between eight viral genes
    Zeng, Hong-Li, Dichio, Vito, Rodrı́guez Horta, Edwin, Thorell, Kaisa, and Aurell, Erik
    Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 2020
    Abs Bib HTML

    We apply Direct Coupling Analysis (DCA) to a large dataset of SARS-CoV-2 genomes to infer the strongest epistatic coupling between different genes. Our analysis unveils significant interactions between the loci in the viral genes ORF3a and nsp2, nsp12, and nsp6, as well as between ORF8 and nsp4, and among the loci in the genes nsp2, nsp13, and nsp14.
